Boston Outside Film Festival

The Boston Outdoor Film Festival (BOFF), is an International, competitive festival. BOFF mission is to provide a public forum in Boston in order to advance public interest in films and the independent production of films. To draw worldwide attention to Boston as a center for cinema. To encourage the rights of all Boston residents to access and experience the power of independent filmmaking, and to promote artistic excellence and the creative freedom of artists without censure. Watch It Outside Boston, is a not-for-profit organization.

Overview

A rat named Remy dreams of becoming a great French chef despite his family's wishes and the obvious problem of being a rat in a decidedly rodent-phobic profession. When fate places Remy in the sewers of Paris, he finds himself ideally situated beneath a restaurant made famous by his culinary hero, Auguste Gusteau. Venue

Pubic Garden - 4 Charles St, Boston, MA 02108

The Public Garden, also known as Boston Public Garden, is a large park in the heart of Boston, Massachusetts, adjacent to Boston Common. It is a part of the Emerald Necklace system of parks, and is bounded by Charles Street and Boston Common to the east, Beacon Street to the north, Arlington Street and Back Bay to the west, and Boylston Street to the south.
